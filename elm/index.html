Valenite

Element Name: Valenite Symbol: Vl Atomic Number: 121 Atomic Mass (approx.): 321 u Protons: 121 Neutrons: 200 Electrons: 121

Category: Transition Metal. Ferrous or Non-Ferrous: Non-ferrous but strongly magnetic State at Room Temperature: Solid Color/Appearance: Reflective light blue, metallic sheen

Melting Point: Low (easily melts under intense heat) Boiling Point: Very high (remains solid under most temperatures) Temperature Stability Range: Stable through extreme cold and high temperatures Density: Very high (notably heavy) Hardness: Sturdy, resistant to stress Malleability: Easily shaped and molded Conductivity: Moderate (used in controlled tech applications) Magnetism: Naturally magnetic; maintains magnetic polarity Radioactivity: None (biologically safe and inert)

Corrosion Resistance: Completely corrosion-proof Affinity: Bonds only with Cerathium Oxidation States: None observed Solubility: Insoluble in all known liquids Stability: Highly stable, maintains structure indefinitely

Date of Discovery: 1979 Discovered By: A PSC field agent Cultural/Scientific Impact: Revolutionized cancer treatment and prosthetic development; considered a national research priority material.
